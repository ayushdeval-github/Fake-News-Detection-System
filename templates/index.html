TruthLens — Fake News Detection System
🔬 AI-Powered Research Project
Combating Misinformation with Machine Learning
TruthLens is an end-to-end fake news detection platform that combines classical machine learning, deep learning, and large language models to identify misinformation in real time — with a focus on both international and Indian regional news.
📌 Project Overview

The rapid spread of misinformation across digital platforms poses a significant threat to public discourse, democratic processes, and societal trust. TruthLens addresses this challenge by deploying a multi-model AI pipeline that analyzes news text and delivers a verdict — Real or Fake — along with a confidence score and a natural-language explanation.

The system is trained on the ISOT Fake News Dataset (44,898 articles from Reuters and PolitiFact), augmented with 3.8 million real Indian headlines from the Times of India corpus and a curated set of 227 state- and city-level Indian news samples — making it one of the few fake news detectors with strong coverage of Indian regional content.

⚙️ Technical Architecture
📊
Logistic Regression
TF-IDF vectorization with MaxAbsScaler. Fastest inference (<50ms). Calibrated threshold for domain-shifted text.
🧠
LSTM Neural Network
Bidirectional LSTM with Embedding layer, SpatialDropout, and Dense(2, softmax) output. Trained with EarlyStopping.
🤖
BERT Transformer
Full bert-base-uncased fine-tuned for sequence classification. Bidirectional context understanding. Highest accuracy.
💬
Groq LLaMA 3 70B
LLaMA-3.3-70B-Versatile via Groq API. Provides human-readable explanation and cross-validates the ML verdict.
🗄️
Flask + MongoDB
RESTful API backend with JWT authentication. MongoDB Atlas stores user accounts and full search history.
🧹
NLP Preprocessing
Dateline removal, stop-word filtering, lemmatization, and domain-specific augmentation for Indian news coverage.

� How It Works

When a user submits a news article, the text is first preprocessed — HTML tags, URLs, and Reuters datelines are stripped, stop words are removed, and tokens are lemmatized. The cleaned text is then passed to the selected ML model (LR, LSTM, or BERT) which outputs a probability score. Simultaneously, the raw text is sent to Groq's LLaMA 3 70B API for a contextual explanation. The final verdict combines both signals: if they agree, confidence is boosted; if they disagree, the Groq verdict takes precedence as it has broader world knowledge.
